How to Stay Focused While Studying

Focus isn’t just willpower. It’s the result of how you set up your environment, your session length, and what you allow to interrupt you.

1. Short focused blocks beat marathon sessions

Aim for 25–40 minutes of focused work followed by a 5–10 minute break. During the focus block, you only work on one task.

2. Remove obvious distractions before you start

  • Put your phone in another room, or at least out of reach.
  • Close all tabs that are not related to the task.
  • Tell people around you you’ll be busy for the next 30 minutes.

3. Create a “start ritual”

Do the same small sequence before every study session: water, open notes, write your first task on paper. This trains your brain to recognize “it’s time to focus now.”
